Internal_Operations_Platform_Scope_v0.1
1. Purpose
Define a bounded internal build track for an operations platform in a pre-operational state.
Support the currently active minimum operating model with internal structure and traceability.
Preserve forward compatibility with later activation of broader delivery/operations architecture, without activating it now.
2. Status
Classification: internal, pre-operational, non-public.
This document is a planning scope artifact only, not an activation decision.
The platform is not a production system, not a public offering, and not a formal operating authority.
3. What the platform may support now
Internal intake capture and work tracking for current operations.
Basic internal workflow visibility (request, owner, state, handoff).
Internal storage of artifacts, assumptions, and decision records.
Lightweight consistency checks that assist current execution.
Readiness-oriented structure that does not replace the active minimum operating model.
4. What the platform must not imply publicly or operationally yet
No public claim of a launched platform, productized capability, or client-facing system.
No implied expansion of service scope, delivery model, or operational maturity.
No implied guarantees, certifications, compliance authority, or production readiness.
No internal assumption that this platform supersedes current active operating practice.
No contractual, legal, or commercial claim derived from platform existence.
5. Minimum modules to build first
Intake Register (Internal): capture incoming internal work items and status.
Execution Board (Internal): track lifecycle states and ownership across active work.
Artifact & Decision Log (Internal): store outputs, rationale, and change notes.
Boundary Flags (Internal): mark pre-operational vs activation-requiring actions.
6. Activation boundary
Platform remains pre-operational until explicit written internal activation authorization.
Activation criteria and governance are separate and out of scope for v0.1.
Before activation, outputs are advisory/internal only.
No public publication, client reliance, or operating-model replacement is allowed pre-activation.
7. Deferred components
Full technical specification and production architecture.
Broader delivery/operations integrations and orchestration.
Advanced automation, scaling, resilience, and monitoring layers.
External interfaces, client-facing workflows, and reporting surfaces.
Formal compliance/audit control frameworks and active-state implications pending separate authorization.
